- Pikaday, once a [JavaScript date picker component,](https://github.com/Pikaday/Pikaday) has been relaunched. Not as yet another component, but as ["a friendly guide to front-end date pickers"](https://pikaday.dbushell.com/)---A great collection of UI patterns and approaches to tackle this notoriously complicated component with relatively simple building blocks.

- After proclaiming that "AI coding sucks" [last month,](/notes/2025-10) CJ is following up with [another video](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=g6_e5Mj7Hzw) talking about his experience of going cold turkey on AI: what he learned about coding, craft, and how he's going to integrate AI into his workflows going forward.

- CSS Subgrid is both powerful for layout and at times mystifying. Josh has written an [approachable guide](https://www.joshwcomeau.com/css/subgrid/) with many interactive examples to help you make sense of it. 

- [Importing JSON](https://developer.mozilla.org/en-US/docs/Web/JavaScript/Reference/Statements/import/with#importing_json_modules_with_the_type_attribute) is now Baseline Newly Available. [Jake explains](https://jakearchibald.com/2025/importing-vs-fetching-json/) why you might still want to `fetch` it instead. Read to learn more about caching, memory leaks, and how browsers handle modules.

- URLs are not just technical resource specifiers, but also [UI elements](https://www.hanselman.com/blog/urls-are-ui) and [state containers.](https://alfy.blog/2025/10/31/your-url-is-your-state.html)---and as such deserve more consideration than they usually get. Did you know that in the Portal team, our designers are involved in how our URLs are structured?

### Notable releases:

- [Vitest 4.0,](https://vitest.dev/blog/vitest-4) featuring stable [browser mode](https://vitest.dev/guide/browser/#browser-mode) and [visual regression testing.](https://vitest.dev/guide/browser/visual-regression-testing.html) Learn more about browser mode in [this talk from ViteConf](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VFYqwXPgJFw).

- [Storybook 10,](https://storybook.js.org/blog/storybook-10/) reducing installation size by another 29%, and shipping improved mocking and testing.

- [Node 24.11,](https://nodejs.org/en/blog/release/v24.11.0) marking the transition to LTS. This version will receive updates through to April 2028.

- [Claude Opus 4.5,](https://www.anthropic.com/news/claude-opus-4-5) the first AI model in a while that feels significantly more useful and capable than the rest.

- [Biome 2.3,](https://biomejs.dev/blog/biome-v2-3/) with support for Vue, Svelte, Astro, and Tailwind.

- [GitHub Universe,](https://github.com/events/universe/recap) announcing---unsurprisingly---lots of AI stuff, but also GitHub Code Quality, a competitor to SonarCloud.

- [Angular 21,](https://blog.angular.dev/announcing-angular-v21-57946c34f14b) dropping the dreaded zone.js in favor of signals, and shipping [Angular Aria,](https://angular.dev/guide/aria/overview) an experimental set of "headless components with accessibility as a priority".
