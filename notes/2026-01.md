- [Pre-commit hooks are fundamentally broken](https://jyn.dev/pre-commit-hooks-are-fundamentally-broken/) (personally, I disable them locally in my [lefthook-local.yml](https://lefthook.dev/examples/lefthook-local.html) and rely on our pipeline to discover the occasional issue ðŸ¤­):

> Pre-commit hooks are a fundamentally broken idea. Code does not exist in isolation. Commits that are local to a developer machine do not ever go through CI. Commits don't even necessarily mean that that the code is ready to publish. [...] More than that, pre-commit hooks are preventing you from saving your work. There should be a really, really good reason to prevent you from saving your work, and IMO "doesn't pass the test suite" is not that.

- Learn [how to use Dev Containers,](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=kPMA9cnpScU) a tool and specification supported by many editors. Dev Containers allow you to run your entire project and related tooling in a Docker-based sandbox, making the setup less dependent on your local machine, and offering (some) protection against malicious dependencies or [coding agents going wild.](https://code.claude.com/docs/en/devcontainer)
