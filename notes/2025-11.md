- **Pikaday,** once a [JavaScript date picker component,](https://github.com/Pikaday/Pikaday) has been relaunched as ["a friendly guide to front-end date pickers"](https://pikaday.dbushell.com/)---a great collection of UI patterns and approaches to tackle this notoriously complicated component with relatively simple building blocks.

- CJ [follows up on "AI coding sucks".](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=g6_e5Mj7Hzw) He shares his thoughts after **not using AI for 30 days,** what he learned about coding and craft, and how he's going to integrate AI into his workflow going forward.

- **CSS Subgrid** is both powerful for layout and at times mystifying. Josh has written an [approachable guide](https://www.joshwcomeau.com/css/subgrid/) with many interactive examples to help you make sense of it. 

- [Importing JSON](https://developer.mozilla.org/en-US/docs/Web/JavaScript/Reference/Statements/import/with#importing_json_modules_with_the_type_attribute) is now *Baseline Newly Available.* [Jake explains](https://jakearchibald.com/2025/importing-vs-fetching-json/) why you might still want to `fetch` it instead. Read to learn about **caching, memory leaks, and how browsers handle modules.**

- URLs are not just technical descriptors, but also [UI elements](https://www.hanselman.com/blog/urls-are-ui) and application state. Here are some [considerations to help you **design great URLs.**](https://alfy.blog/2025/10/31/your-url-is-your-state.html) (Did you know that our designers in the Portal team are involved in specifying URLs?)

- "The bad news is that [your software is rotting.](https://terminal.ahumanfuture.co/posts/2025-04-19/your-software-is-rotting/) The good news is that there are many simple but powerful things we can do to slow the process to a crawl."
